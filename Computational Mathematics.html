Computational Mathematics

Deriving \(\pi\) with RNG

From geometry and other lower level math classes, you know that the area of a rectangle satisfies the equation \(A=lw\), and likewise for a circle, \(A=\pi r^2\). Using these facts, as well as the python random module, write a program to estimate \(\pi\), the transcendental circle constant.

Nota Bene! Think carefully about squares and circles. If you were to inscribe a circle of radius 1 inside a square of side length 2, what fraction of the square does the circle eclipse?
