Mechanics & Thermo

Physical Measurements

  • Length is measured in meters (m).
  • Mass is measured in kilograms (kg). Mass is a measure of the inertia of an object, sometimes understand as the quantity of matter in the objects. Mass is NOT weight.
  • Time is measured in seconds (s).

Kinematics Equations

  • \(x(t)=\frac{1}{2}at^2+v_0t+x_0\) relates \(t\) and \(x\).
  • \(v(t)=at+v_0\) relates \(t\) and \(v\).
  • \(v(t)^2-{v_0}^2=2a(x(t)-x_0)\) relates \(x\) and \(v\).

Other Kinematics Ideas

  • \(\bar{v}=\frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}\) and \(\bar{a}=\frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}\) denote average velocity and acceleration.
  • \(v= \frac{dx}{dt}\) and \(a= \frac{dv}{dt}\) denote instantaneous velocity and acceleration.
  • \(\bar{s}=\frac{\text{distance}}{\Delta t}\) denotes average speed.

Forces

In simplist terms, a force is a push or pull, thus naturally, forces are vectors. The unit of force is called the Newton, where \(1 N = 1 kg \cdot \frac{1 m}{s^2}\).

Newton's Laws of Motion

  1. Every object continues in its state of uniform velocity as long as no net force acts on it.
  2. The relationship between force, mass, and acceleration, \(\Sigma \vec{F}=m\vec{a}\). Newton's first law is actually just a special case of this, where \(\Sigma \vec{F}=0\) and thus \(\vec{a}=0\).
  3. If one object exerts a force on a second object, the second exerts back a force of equal magnitude in the opposite direction on the first. This can be expressed through the equation \(\vec{F}_{ab}=-\vec{F}_{ba}\) where the first subscript is the object on which the force is being exerted, while the second is the source.

Weight

Weight is the force exerted on an object by gravity.

\[\vec{F}_G = -m\vec{g}\]

Near the surface of the earth, \(g=9.80 m/s^2\)
